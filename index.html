YOU ARE EVERY PAGES

A poem for Chris by Honey

The ink would run dry before she could finish a single page, as though there was never anything worth writing at all.

Her pencil would snap beneath the weight of endless revisions, and failed attempts at rhymes that never sounded right.

Thoughts lingered through her mind like, as if every blank page seemed to ask the same question...

What is there to write?

But in the most unexpected way, she met Chris.

And suddenly, the fogged in her mind disappeared.

He had always been the poem she was searching for without knowing it.

For how could she ever run out of things to say about him?

His eyes, she could write thousands of words about his eyes alone. It is like a green map with traces of directions as if they held places she has to discover yet each time she looked into them, she found herself wonderfully lost.

She could write about his handsome face, it is like a moon, it changes through different phases yet beautiful in every form. And when a smile curved upon his lips, it became a beautiful crescent.

She could write about the gentleness of his heart no naked eyes could see, the way kindness seemed to just naturally flow into his very nature, the way he care for others, the love he pours for the people he dears.

She could write thousands of words about his voice too. There was something about his Aussie accent; he could make even the most ordinary words sound beautiful. His voice felt like a forgotten song she had not heard in years, yet somehow still knew by heart. She could listen to him for hours and hours and never grow tired of it.

She was enchanted by him so effortlessly that it felt less like meeting a stranger and more like finding someone her soul had been searching for all along.

As if somewhere beyond time,
before names and places and years,
their souls had already known one another.

And at last, after waiting for what felt like forever, they met.

Suddenly, the words came easily.

The rhymes no longer mattered.

The structure no longer mattered.

The stanzas no longer mattered.

Because she realized she could fill an entire book with his name between the lines.

A book where every chapter held a different memory,
a different feeling,
a different reason to admire him.

He was never just a poem.

He was every pages she longed to write.

And now, at last,

she had.
